Nomad Credit — Modernizing the Student Financing Experience
TL;DR

Redesigned Nomad Credit’s student-facing ecosystem to create a more modern, trustworthy, and conversion-friendly financing experience for international students.

The project covered the public website, loan discovery flows, student dashboard, Doc Pilot document upload system, and supporting CRM workflows used by loan managers internally.

The redesign focused on improving clarity across complex financial journeys while centralizing fragmented document collection workflows previously spread across WhatsApp and email.

Reduced friction in document collection workflows

Overview

Nomad Credit is a student financing platform designed to help international students discover and apply for education loans, scholarships, and related financial products.

I worked alongside the PM to redesign and modernize the platform’s overall experience while improving operational workflows connected to the loan journey.

The project expanded across multiple interconnected systems:

Website revamp
Student dashboard
Loan discovery experience
Scholarship tool
Application flow
Doc Pilot document collection system
Internal CRM improvements

Rather than redesigning isolated screens, the goal was to create a more connected ecosystem that balanced trust, clarity, and operational efficiency.

Problem

The existing platform no longer reflected the scale and maturity of the brand. The experience felt visually outdated, inconsistent across touchpoints, and lacked the clarity expected from modern fintech products.

At the same time, the loan journey itself involved high-trust decision making. Students were expected to compare financial products, understand eligibility, manage documentation, and stay updated throughout the process — often while navigating unfamiliar systems.

The operational side faced similar friction.

Document collection was heavily fragmented across WhatsApp and email threads, making it difficult for both students and loan managers to track required paperwork efficiently.

This created:

fragmented communication
delayed document collection
operational inefficiencies
reduced visibility into application progress
inconsistent user experiences across platforms

The challenge wasn’t only modernizing the interface — it was designing a more connected and trustworthy ecosystem.

Goals

The redesign focused on four primary goals:

Improve responsiveness across devices
Modernize the brand perception
Increase clarity across complex financial workflows
Create consistency across the ecosystem

Operationally, the project also aimed to:

centralize document collection
reduce dependency on scattered communication channels
streamline follow-up workflows internally
Research & Direction

The project began with an audit of the existing platform to identify usability gaps, inconsistencies, and outdated interaction patterns.

We then explored competitor and reference platforms across fintech, SaaS, and travel-tech products to define a more modern direction for the experience.

Two key references shaped the design approach:

Marketplace-style exploration

Inspired by platforms like Amazon, the loan discovery experience focused on helping students browse and compare lenders more intuitively instead of forcing them into rigid flows immediately.

Trust-focused onboarding

Platforms like Atlys influenced the way trust signals, guidance, and clarity were introduced throughout the journey.

This combination helped create an experience that balanced discoverability with reassurance.

Design Process

The workflow remained highly iterative throughout the project.

1. Platform Audit

Reviewed the existing ecosystem to identify friction points, outdated UI patterns, and inconsistencies.

2. Competitor Research

Analyzed modern fintech and marketplace experiences to establish a stronger visual and UX direction.

3. High-Fidelity Explorations

Multiple high-fidelity directions were explored early to evaluate:

visual tone
information hierarchy
lender discovery patterns
dashboard structures
interaction clarity
4. Iterations with PM

Designs evolved through continuous feedback loops with the PM to align business priorities with usability needs.

5. Stakeholder Feedback & Handoff

Finalized flows and interfaces were refined based on stakeholder discussions before developer handoff.

Doc Pilot System

One of the biggest operational challenges involved document collection.

Previously, required loan documents were often shared across fragmented communication channels such as WhatsApp and email. This created inefficiencies for both students and loan managers while reducing visibility into application progress.

The Doc Pilot system was designed to centralize this workflow into a single structured experience.

Students could securely upload required documents through guided flows while loan managers could trigger magic links directly from the CRM. These links could then be shared through email or WhatsApp depending on the student’s preferred communication channel.

The system aimed to:

reduce fragmented communication
improve organization
centralize uploads
speed up document collection
improve operational visibility
increase trust through platform-based uploads
Workflow Improvements
Reduced dependency on manual document tracking
Faster document submission turnaround
Better visibility into pending uploads
More structured follow-up workflows for loan managers

Rather than forcing students away from familiar communication habits, the workflow integrated with them more naturally.

This became one of the most operationally impactful parts of the redesign.

Reflection

This project reinforced how important trust is within financial experiences.

Students were not simply interacting with forms — they were making high-stakes decisions connected to their education and future. Designing for clarity, reassurance, and operational simplicity became just as important as visual modernization.

It also highlighted the importance of designing systems rather than isolated screens. The most meaningful improvements emerged when student workflows, communication channels, and internal operations were treated as connected parts of the same ecosystem.
